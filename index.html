About Me

Ari Gelbard is a current student in the Data Science and Analytics program at Georgetown University. He received his bachelor's degree in Economics and Mathematics with a minor in Global Poverty from the University of Maryland, College Park in 2022. He graduated with honors after writing an undergraduate thesis on the effect of property rights on income inequality.

After graduating, he worked for the Federal Reserve Board of Governors as a research assistant (RA) for three years in the Household and Business Spending section of the Division of Research and Statistics (R&S). While at The Board, his work consisted of managing the GDP and investment databases, running econometric models and analyzing real-time economic data, contributing to and writing memos pertaining to the staff's forecasts, and working with economists on a variety of research and policy projects. He also received the 2024 Division Director's Award for his work on a project that summarized the contributions of the R&S division to the field of economic measurement.

In his free time, Ari enjoys playing board games, cooking, playing tennis, and bowling.
