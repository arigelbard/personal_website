About Me

I am a current student in the Data Science and Analytics program at Georgetown University. I received my bachelor's degree in Economics and Mathematics with a minor in Global Poverty from the University of Maryland, College Park in 2022, graduating with honors after writing an undergraduate thesis on the effect of property rights on income inequality.

After graduating, I spent three years at the Federal Reserve Board of Governors as a Senior Research Assistant in the Division of Research and Statistics. My work focused on forecasting US GDP and private fixed investment, maintaining macroeconomic databases, contributing to policy memos, and collaborating with economists on a variety of research projects. I also received the 2024 Division Director's Award for my work on a project about the contributions of the Federal reserve to the field of economic measurement.

In my free time, I enjoy playing board games, cooking, playing tennis, and bowling.
